This sum is called a Riemann Sum approximation for area. If the function is well behaved (for us, continuous), then we can keep taking finer and finer partitions for better and better approximations. This process can be extended into some kind of limit process. In the limit, we expect a perfect calculation of area.

\begin{equation*} A = \lim_{n \rightarrow \infty} \sum_{k=1}^n f(x_k^*) \frac{b-a}{n} \end{equation*}
