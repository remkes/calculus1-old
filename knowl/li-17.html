Item. Algebraic Definition.

An ellipse (centred at the origin) is described by the equation \(\frac{x^2}{a^2} + \frac{y^2}{b^2} = 1\text{.}\) Assuming that \(a>b\text{,}\) the foci are at \((\sqrt{a^2-b^2},0)\) and \((-\sqrt{a^2-b^2},0)\text{.}\) From the number \(a\) and \(b\text{,}\) which ever is larger is called the semi-major axis which ever is smaller is called semi-minor axis. These two numbers cannot be equal, since that would give a circle.
