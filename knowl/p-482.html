These are both quotient rule derivatives. I'll not write the general form and labelling here, but proceed with the specific calculation.

\begin{equation*} \frac{d}{dx} \frac{\cos x}{x} + \frac{\sin x}{x} = \frac{x \frac{d}{dx} \cos x - \cos x \frac{d}{dx} x}{\cos^2 x} + \frac{x \frac{d}{dx} \sin x - \sin x \frac{d}{dx} x}{\sin^2 x} \end{equation*}

Then we need to all four of the specific derivatives, which are all known derivatives.

\begin{equation*} = \frac{-x \sin x - \cos x}{\cos^2} + \frac{x \cos x - \sin x}{\sin^2 x} \end{equation*}

Some change in expression could be made -- common denominator for both fractions, or splitting up numerators. None of them seems to me to create any substantially simpler expression, so I'm content to leave the answer in this form.
