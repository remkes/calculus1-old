We use asymptotic analysis for limits a \(x \rightarrow \infty\text{.}\) The asymptotic order of the numerator is \(x\text{.}\) The asymptotic order of the denominator is \(x\text{.}\) The sine function isn't in our list, because it is bounded -- it doesn't grow at all. Since the asymptotic orders are the same, we compare the leading coefficients.

\begin{equation*} \lim_{x \rightarrow \infty} \frac{5 + \ln x}{\sqrt{1+ 7x}} = \frac{1}{5} \end{equation*}
