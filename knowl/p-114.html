If \(a\) is a positive real number, then functions of the form \(f(x) = a^x\) are called exponential functions. They differ from roots and polynomials in that the variable is in the exponent. This distinction is very important but easy to confuse: \(f(x) = x^a\) is an algebraic function but \(f(x) = a^x\) is an exponential function, which is not algebraic. The laws of exponents (see the formula sheets) help us work with exponential functions.
