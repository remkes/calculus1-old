Finally, we have evaluation for some common sums. These expressions will be used, along with the previously stated rules, to calculate integrals in the next section.

\begin{align*} \sum_{k=1}^n 1 \amp = n\\ \sum_{k=1}^n k \amp = \frac{n(n+1)}{2}\\ \sum_{k=1}^n k^2 \amp = \frac{n(n+1)(2n+1)}{6}\\ \sum_{k=1}^n k^3 \amp = \left(\frac{n(n+1)}{2} \right)^2 \end{align*}
