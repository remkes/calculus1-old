Everything we've defined so far comes together to form the large family refered to as the elementary functions. We can think of elementary functions as those function for which we have a familiar and succinct notation. Everything we've defined so far, as well as any combination of these functions, is an elementary function. As we will see later, calculus gives us methods for defining many non-elementary functions. Depending on how far you proceed through the calculus stream, you will see a few or many of these non-elementary functions.
