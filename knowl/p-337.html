In quotient limits, \(f\) and \(g\) may be more complicated than the simple functions in our asymptotic ranking. We have a two useful rules to help us work with \(f\) and \(g\) which are combinations of pieces of various asymptotic order.

  • If \(f = f_1 + f_2 + f_3 + \ldots + f_n\) then the asymptotic order of \(f\) is the maximum of the asymptotic order of the \(f_i\text{.}\) This means that in a sum or difference, we only need to consider the fastest growing pieces. We can simply ignore all the rest.

  • If \(f_1 \lt g_1\) and either \(f_2 \lt g_2\) or \(f_2\) and \(g_2\) have the same asymptotic order, then \(f_1 f_2 \lt g_1 g_2\text{.}\) Basically, this says that relationships of asymptotic order are preserved in products.
