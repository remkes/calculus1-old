To calculate the phase line, we need the zeros of the right side of the equation. This is a cubic: we can try to look for the roots by inspection or as a computer for the roots. In an case, we find that \(p=1\text{,}\) \(p=2\) and \(p=3\) are the roots. We label these points on the phase line. To determine the trajectories, we check the value of the right side of the equation between the steady states.
This lets us classify the steady states.
