The derivative is the rate of change of the function, so for this model, \(\frac{dp}{dt}\) is the growth rate of the population. The average annual increase in the data above is \(20\)% growth. That means that the growth rate (the amount of population added in a year) is \(\frac{20}{100}\) (or \(\frac{1}{5}\)) times the current population. We can express this as an equation.

\begin{equation*} \frac{dp}{dt} = \frac{1}{5} p(t) \end{equation*}
