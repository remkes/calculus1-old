Linearity splits up the sum over addition and subtraction and allows us to pull out the constants. In each piece, the index and bounds of the sum stay the same.

\begin{equation*} \sum_{i=0}^{44} (5i^3 - 2i^2 - 19i + 19) = 5 \sum_{i=0}^{44} i^3 - 2 \sum_{i=0}^{44} i^2 - 19 \sum_{i=0}^{44} i + 19 \sum_{i=0}^{44} 1 \end{equation*}
