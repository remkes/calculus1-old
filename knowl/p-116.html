The inverse of the exponential function is the logarithm. If the exponential has base \(a\text{,}\) its logarithm is written \(\log_a x\text{.}\) However, the inverse of the exponential \(f(x) = e^x\) is instead written \(\ln x\) and called the natural logarithm. We will almost exclusively deal with the natural logarithm in calculus. Logarithms (in any base) can only act on positive numbers.
