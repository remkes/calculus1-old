There are several ways in which the limit can diverge. The statement

\begin{equation*} \lim_{x \rightarrow a} f(x) = \infty \end{equation*}

means that as \(x\) gets closer and to \(a\text{,}\) the function value \(f(x)\) gets larger and larger without bound. The statement

\begin{equation*} \lim_{x \rightarrow a} f(x) = -\infty \end{equation*}

means that as \(x\) gets closer and to \(a\text{,}\) the function value \(f(x)\) becomes a larger and larger negative value without bound. The statement

\begin{equation*} \lim_{x \rightarrow a} f(x) \hspace{1cm} \text{ DNE } \end{equation*}

means that the limit does not exist; it doesn't approach any number at all.
