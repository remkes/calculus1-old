We can explain the notation. The sigma of sigma notation gets replaced with \(\int\text{,}\) which is the integral sign. The bounds \(k=1\) and \(n\) get replaced with the endpoint of the interval, \(a\) and \(b\) respectively. The \(f(x^*_k)\) just becomes the function \(f(x)\text{.}\) Finally, the term \(\frac{b-a}{n}\) gets infinitesimally small as \(n \rightarrow \infty\text{,}\) so it becomes the infinitesimal term \(dx\text{.}\)
