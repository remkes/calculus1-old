It is natural to ask if \(f\) is continuous at \(x=a\text{.}\) In order to investigate this, we need to look at the function value and the limits from both sides. The function is continuous if all three are the same.

\begin{equation*} \lim_{x \rightarrow a^-} f(x) = \lim_{x \rightarrow a^+} f(x) = f(a) \end{equation*}
