Let's say we want the slope of the tangent line at a point \((a,f(a))\) on the graph of a function. We can take \(a\) as one point to define a secant line and let \(x\) be the other point (with \(x \neq a\)). Then the slope of the secant line is the difference in output (\(f(x) - f(a)\)) dividing by the difference in input (\(x-a\)).

\begin{equation*} \frac{f(x) - f(a)}{x-a} \end{equation*}
