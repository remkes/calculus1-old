What happens for these gasses at high pressures? It's difficult to solve directly for volume, but we can analyze the equation in its current form. If the term involving pressure grows very large, the term only involving volume must grow to zero, so that the product remains the same. That happens when \(V\) is very close to the value \(nb\text{.}\)

\begin{equation*} \lim_{P \rightarrow \infty} V = nb \end{equation*}
