The most familiar way to put functions together is through the conventional operations of arithmetic: addition, subtraction, multiplication, division and exponentiation. If \(f\) and \(g\) are functions, then \(f+g\text{,}\) \(f-g\text{,}\) \(f \cdot g\text{,}\) \(\frac{f}{g}\) and \(f^g\) are all functions. They may have additional domain restrictions, such as avoiding the roots of \(g\) in \(\frac{f}{g}\text{.}\) We call these combinations pointwise operations on function.
