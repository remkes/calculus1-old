The first type of non-algebraic functions are trigonometric functions. Sine and cosine have the familiar sinusoidal wave shape: infinitenly many oscilation which repeat perfectly with some period. Sine and cosine waves can be analyzed by their amplitude and period. The other trigonometric functions (tangent, cotangent, secant and cosecant) are also periodic, but they have undefined points and vertical asymptotes at regular intervals.
