To calculate the phase line, we need the zeros of the right side of the equation. This is a quadratic and it factors as \((p-6)(p-3)\text{.}\) Therefore, we have steady states at \(p=3\) and \(p=6\text{.}\) We label these points on the phase line. To determine the trajectories, we check the value of the right side of the equation between the steady states.
