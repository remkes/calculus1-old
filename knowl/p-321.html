Asymptotic analysis interprets limit at infinity as a measurement of the growth of functions. The functions \(f_1(x) = x\text{,}\) \(f_2(x) = x^2\) and \(f_3(x) = e^x\) all get very large as \(x\) gets very large; they all grow. Asymptotic analysis asks which of these functions grows faster.
