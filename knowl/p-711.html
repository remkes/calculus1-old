We have two critical points, \(x=2\) and \(x=-3\text{.}\) The domain of the function is \(\RR\text{,}\) so the intervals are \((-\infty,-3)\text{,}\) \((-3,2)\) and \((2,\infty)\text{.}\) We check the sign of the derivative on these intevals; we do this by simply choosing any point in the interval and evaluated. The following table shows the intervals and the behaviour of the derivative on each interval.

\begin{align*} \amp (-\infty, -3) \amp \amp (-3,2) \amp \amp (2,\infty) \\ \amp f^\prime(-4) = 36 \amp \amp f^\prime(0) = -36 \amp \amp f^\prime(3) = 36 \\ \amp f^\prime(-4) \gt 0 \amp \amp f^\prime(0) \lt 0 \amp \amp f^\prime(3) \gt 0 \\ \amp f \text{ is increasing.} \amp \amp f \text{ is decreasing.} \amp \amp f \text{ is increasing.} \end{align*}
