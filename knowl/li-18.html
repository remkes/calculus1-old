Item. Intrinsic Definition.

Given a positive real number \(r\text{,}\) a point \(p\) and a line \(L\text{,}\) the parabola is the set of all points in the plane which are equidistant to the point \(p\) and the line \(L\text{.}\) The point \(p\) is called the focus and the line \(L\) is called the directrix.
