In asymptotic analysis, we start with a quotient limit.

\begin{equation*} \lim_{x \rightarrow \pm \infty} \frac{f(x)}{g(x)} \end{equation*}

  • If this limit is \(0\text{,}\) then we say that \(g\) has greater asymptotic order than \(f\text{.}\) Alternatively, we say that \(g\) grows faster than or \(g\) dominates \(f\) as \(x \rightarrow \infty\text{.}\)

  • If this limit is \(\infty\text{,}\) then we say that \(f\) has greater asymptotic order than \(g\text{.}\) Alternatively, we say that \(f\) grows faster than or \(f\) dominates \(g\text{.}\)

  • If this limit is finite but non-zero, we say that \(f\) and \(g\) have the same asymptotic order. Alternatively, .we say that \(f\) and \(g\) grow at the same asymptotic rate and neither dominates.
