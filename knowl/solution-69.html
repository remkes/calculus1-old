Solution 5.5.11.1.

We use asymptotic analysis for limits a \(x \rightarrow \infty\text{.}\) The asymptotic order of the numerator is\(e^{7x}\text{.}\) The asymptotic order of the denominator is \(e^{8x}\text{.}\) We know for exponential functions (always base e, of course) that a higher exponential coefficient means a higher asymptotic order. Therefore, the denominator has higher asymptotic order and the limit converges to 0.
