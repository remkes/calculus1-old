If both \(f(x)\) and \(g(x)\) approach \(\pm \infty\text{,}\) this limit is called an indeterminate form of type \(\infty - \infty\text{.}\) For this type, we want to use common denominator, conjugates or other algegraic tricks to reduce it to a limit of type \(\frac{\infty}{\infty}\) or type \(\frac{0}{0}\text{.}\)
