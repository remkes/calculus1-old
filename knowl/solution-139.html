Solution 8.4.22.1.

The antiderivative of \(\sin x\) is \((-\cos x)\text{.}\) For definite integrals, after we find the antiderivative, we evaluate it on the bounds. (We don't need the constant of integral in definite integrals, since it would just cancel off when we evaluate on the bounds.)

\begin{equation*} \int_0^{\pi} \sin x dx = -\cos x \Big|_0^{\pi} = -\cos (\pi) - (- \cos (0)) = -(-1) - (-1) = 2 \end{equation*}
