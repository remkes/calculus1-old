A line is the locus of any equation where the variables \(x\) and \(y\) show up multiplied by constants and then added to another constant. Other than multiplication by a constant, no other operations are performed on the variables: no exponents, no roots, no functions, nothing else. These equations, since they produce lines, are called linear equations. Here are some examples.

\begin{align*} y \amp = 7x - 9 \\ 5y + 4x - 10 \amp = 0\\ \frac{1}{5} y - \frac{2}{7} x - \frac{5}{17} \amp = 0 \\ \sqrt{7}y \amp = \pi x + \frac{1}{\sqrt{19}} \end{align*}

In all these equations, all we have done is multiplied each varaible by some constant and added an additional constant. By adding and subtracting terms, we can put some of the variables or the constant on the right or left side of the equation, as we see fit.
