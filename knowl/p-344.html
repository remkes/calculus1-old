If \(f(x)\) and \(g(x)\) approach \(\pm \infty\text{,}\) the limit is called an indeterminate form of type \(\frac{\infty}{\infty}\text{.}\) If instead both \(f(x)\) and \(g(x)\) approach \(0\text{,}\) then it is an indeterminate form of type \(\frac{0}{0}\text{.}\) In both cases, we want to use algebra to factor out and cancel off the pieces of the quotients which tends to \(\infty\) or \(0\) to solve the limit.
