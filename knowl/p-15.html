Cartesian coordinate are useful for giving algebraic definitions for various geometric shapes and objects. For an equation in \(x\) and \(y\text{,}\) the coresponding shape consists of all points \((a,b)\) in the plane which satisfy the equation when \(x\) is replaced by \(a\) and \(y\) is replaced by \(b\text{.}\) Such an collection of points is called locus of the equation.
