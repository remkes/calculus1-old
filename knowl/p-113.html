Each trigonometric function has a inverse function. There are two standard notations; the inverse of \(\sin(x)\) is written either as \(\sin^{-1}(x)\) or \(\arcsin(x)\text{.}\) Even though the former notation is familiar from calculators, we will use the latter in these notes to avoid confusions with \(\frac{1}{\sin(x)}\text{.}\)
