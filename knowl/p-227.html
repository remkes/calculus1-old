Let \(f(x)\) be a function and \(a\) a point either in the domain of the function or on the boundary of its domain. Then the statement

\begin{equation*} \lim_{x \rightarrow a} f(x) = L \end{equation*}

means that as \(x\) (the input) gets closer and closer to \(a\text{,}\) f(x) (the output) gets closer and closer to \(L\text{.}\) If such an \(L\) exists, we say the limit of \(f(x)\) exists at \(x=a\text{;}\) we also say that the limit converges. Otherwise we say the limit diverges.
