When we do substitution with definite integrals, we also need to change the bounds. If \(a\) and \(b\) are the bounds in \(x\) and \(u = u(x)\) is the relationship between \(u\) and \(x\text{,}\) then \(u(a)\) and \(u(b)\) will be the bounds in \(u\text{.}\) One nice thing about definite integrals is that we can use these new bounds to evaluate the integral. We don't have to substitute back after we finish.
