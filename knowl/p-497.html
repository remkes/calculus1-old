Another specific example is Hooke's law. This law describes the force caused by a spring; the law states that the force is (negatively) proportional to the distance from equalibrium. If equalibrium is at \(p=0\text{,}\) then \(F = -kp\text{.}\) That gives this differential equation:

\begin{equation*} -kp = m \frac{d^2 p}{dt^2} \end{equation*}
