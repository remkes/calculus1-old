This second definition allows us to see that the derivative is an entirely new function. At each point \(x\) in the domain of \(f\text{,}\) we can ask for the slope of the tangent line. That gives a new function:

\begin{equation*} f^\prime(x) = \frac{df}{dx} = \lim_{h \rightarrow 0} \frac{f(x+h) - f(x)}{h} \end{equation*}
