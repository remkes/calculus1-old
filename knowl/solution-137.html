Solution 8.4.20.1.

We can use linearity to split this into two integrals.

\begin{equation*} \int (x^3-e^x)dx = \int x^3 dx - \int e^x dx \end{equation*}

For the first integral, we have to do the power rule backwards. This increases the exponent and divides by the new exponent. For the second integral, since the exponential function is its own derivative, it is also its own antiderivative.

\begin{equation*} \int x^3 dx - \int e^x dx = \frac{x^4}{4} - e^x + C \end{equation*}
