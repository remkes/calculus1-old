The following calculation is a proof for the Leibniz rule. Note in the second step, we've added and subtracted the same term in the numerator.

\begin{align*} \frac{d}{dx} fg \amp = \lim_{h \rightarrow 0} \frac{f(x+h)g(x+h) - f(x)g(x)}{h}\\ \amp = \lim_{h \rightarrow 0} \frac{f(x+h)g(x+h) - f(x+h) g(x) + f(x+h) g(x) - f(x)g(x)}{h}\\ \amp = \lim_{h \rightarrow 0} f(x+h)\frac{g(x+h) - g(x)}{h} + \lim_{h \rightarrow 0} g(x) \frac{f(x+h) - f(x)}{h}\\ \amp = f(x) \lim_{h \rightarrow 0} \frac{g(x+h) - g(x)}{h} + g(x) \lim_{h \rightarrow 0} \frac{f(x+h) - f(x)}{h}\\ \amp = f(x) \frac{dg}{dx} + f(x) \frac{df}{dx} \end{align*}
