When time is 0, we have \(p(0) = c\text{,}\) so \(c\) is the starting value of the population. The population is linear, with slope \(a-b\text{.}\) This will be positive or negative depending on which parameter is larger. Since the parater \(a\) is positive but we subtract the parameter \(b\text{,}\) \(a\) must have a positive effect on the population and \(b\) must have a negative effect. The simpliest way to interpret this is to assume that \(a\) is a birth rate and \(b\) is a death rate. The population will only grow if the rate of briths exceeds the rate of deaths.
