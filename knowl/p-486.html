The rules we have so far give us the tools to calculate many derivatives. However, they don't address composition. How do we differentiate \(\sin x^2\text{?}\) The rule that addresses derivatives of composed functions is called the chain rule. The chain rule has two notations. Let \(f\) and \(g\) be differentiable functions and consider the composition \(f(g(x))\text{.}\) It is often useful to label \(g(x)\) by a new, temporary variable \(u = g(x)\text{.}\) The vertical line mean evaluate by replaing \(u\) with \(g(x)\text{.}\)

\begin{equation*} \frac{d}{dx} f(g(x)) = f^\prime(g(x)) g^\prime(x) = \left. \frac{df(u)}{du} \right|_{u=g(x)} \frac{dg}{dx} \end{equation*}
