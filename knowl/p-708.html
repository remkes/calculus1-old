In this example, the domain is \(\RR\) and the only critical point is \(x=0\text{.}\) Splitting the domain by that critical point gives us the intervals \((-\infty, 0)\) and \((0,\infty)\text{.}\) On these intervals, the derivative will either be positive or negative, so the function will either be increasing or decreasing respectively. For this example, \(f^\prime(x) = 2x\text{,}\) which is negative on \((-\infty,0)\) and positive on \((0, \infty)\text{;}\) therefore, the function is decreasing on \((-\infty,0)\) and increasing on \((0, \infty)\text{.}\)
