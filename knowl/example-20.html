Example 7.2.2.

Let's see what happens with higher derivatives of a polynomial.

\begin{align*} f(x) \amp = x^5\\ \frac{df}{dx} \amp = 5x^4\\ \frac{d^2f}{dx^2} \amp = 20x^3\\ \frac{d^3f}{dx^3} \amp = 60x^2\\ \frac{d^4f}{dx^4} \amp = 120x\\ \frac{d^5f}{dx^5} \amp = 120\\ \frac{d^6f}{dx^6} \amp = 0 \end{align*}

Each differentiation reduces the degree of the polynomial by one, until we eventually get to zero. If we take enough derivatives of any polynomial, we will eventually reach zero.
